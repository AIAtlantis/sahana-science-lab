Geologic Time
Eras · Periods in order · Extinctions · Life milestones (backbone, eggshells, fruit, land-dwelling, hominids)
🗺️ The Big Structure
Earth's time divided into ERAS → divided into PERIODS
Pre-Era: Precambrian (4,600–541 MYA) — longest eon, simple life only, origin of life
3 Main Eras (oldest→newest): Paleozoic (PZ) → Mesozoic (MZ) → Cenozoic (CZ)
Paleozoic = 7 periods; Mesozoic = 3 periods; Cenozoic = 3 periods
🎯 5 KEY Life Milestones (TEST FOCUS)
🦴 Backbone (vertebrates) → Cambrian Period (jawless fish)
🥚 Eggshells (shelled eggs) → Mississippian Period (first reptiles)
🌸 Fruit (flowering plants) → Cretaceous Period
🦎 Land-dwelling vertebrates → Devonian Period (first amphibians)
🦍 Hominids → Neogene Period (Cenozoic)

Volcanoes
Where they form · 3 types compared · Pyroclastic material · Hot spots · Viscosity
📍 3 Locations Where Volcanoes Form
🔴 Convergent Boundaries — plates COLLIDE → explosive composite volcanoes; subduction pushes plate under
🔵 Divergent Boundaries — plates MOVE APART → magma rises to fill gap; mostly underwater (mid-ocean ridges)
🟡 Hot Spots — STATIONARY magma plumes; plate moves OVER them → volcanic island chains (e.g. Hawaii)
🌊 Key stat: ~90% of all volcanic activity is underwater!
🍯 Viscosity — The Key Concept
Viscosity = how thick/resistant to flow a liquid is
🍯 HIGH viscosity = thick like honey → lava builds UP → steep, explosive volcano
💧 LOW viscosity = flows like water → lava spreads OUT → broad, flat shield volcano
High viscosity lava traps gases → pressure builds → EXPLOSIVE eruptions!

🛡️ ShieldBroad, flat, shield-shapedLOW (flows easily)Basaltic, hotMultiple lava flows, NOT explosive — lava spreads outLARGEST typeHot spots + divergent boundaries
🏔️ CompositeLarge, steep-sided, layeredHIGH (very viscous)Very thick lavaEXPLOSIVE; layers of lava AND ash build upLargeMostly convergent boundaries
📚 Vocabulary Mastery
🔥 Magma — molten rock beneath Earth's surface (underground)
🌋 Lava — molten rock that has erupted onto Earth's surface
🪨 Pyroclastic Material — ALL solid material released in eruption; large = falls near volcano; small ash = travels far with wind
🫧 Hot Spot — stationary plume; plate moves OVER it = chain of volcanic islands (Hawaii!)
🏝️

Hot Spot Fun Fact: The Hawaiian Islands are a perfectly preserved record of a tectonic plate moving! The Big Island is youngest (directly over the hot spot), while Kauai is the oldest and most eroded. An entirely new island called Lōʻihi is currently forming underwater off Hawaii's coast — but won't reach the surface for another ~10,000 years!

The Atmosphere
5 layers · Composition · Air pressure · Coriolis · 3 global winds · 4 fronts · 3 cloud types · Precipitation
📊 5 Layers — Highest to Lowest with KEY Facts
LayerHeightKey FeatureCategory
🛸 Exosphere375–6,200 miSatellites orbit here; atoms escape into space; bounded below by thermopauseUpper
🔆 Thermosphere53–375 miAbsorbs UV + X-ray radiation → LARGE temp increase with height; bounded below by mesopauseUpper
☄️ Mesosphere31–53 miMeteors burn up here → shooting star trails! Bounded below by stratopauseMiddle
🌤️ Stratosphere4–31 miOzone layer lives here; temp INCREASES with height (opposite of troposphere!); bounded below by tropopauseMiddle
🌧️ Troposphere0–11 miALL weather occurs here; where we live; temp DECREASES with height; air density decreases with heightLower
🧪 Atmosphere Composition
78% Nitrogen (N₂) — most abundant by far
21% Oxygen (O₂) — what we breathe
1% Argon + trace gases — ozone (O₃), CO₂, methane, neon
Water vapor: varies 1–4% of atmosphere
💨 Air Pressure Basics
The Sun causes pressure differences — heats air → expands → less dense → lower pressure
Warm areas = LOW pressure; Cold areas = HIGH pressure
High-pressure air flows INTO low-pressure areas → this creates WIND!
Air pressure decreases with altitude because air gets less dense higher up
🌍 Coriolis Effect
Caused by Earth's rotation — deflects wind direction in each hemisphere
🇺🇸 Northern Hemisphere: wind deflects to the RIGHT → storms spin counterclockwise around low pressure
🌏 Southern Hemisphere: wind deflects to the LEFT → storms spin clockwise around low pressure
💨 3 Major Global Wind Patterns
🌴 Trade Winds (~30°N/S) — warm, steady; historically used by sailing ships for trade
🌪️ Prevailing Westerlies (40°–60°) — move major weather systems across continents
🧊 Polar Easterlies (50°–60° near poles) — irregular; weak outside of winter
💨 Jet Stream — most POWERFUL wind belt; narrow, fast, high-altitude band
🌦️ 4 Weather Fronts
❄️ Cold Front — cold air pushes UNDER warm → rises QUICKLY → thunderstorms + cumulus ⛈️
☀️ Warm Front — warm air rises GENTLY over cold → stratus clouds, fog, steady rain 🌧️
⏸️ Stationary Front — neither air mass advances → prolonged clouds + precipitation for days
🔀 Occluded Front — cold overtakes warm → complex weather, storms 🌩️
☁️ Clouds + Precipitation
📜 Stratus — layered, low (<2,000m), causes RAIN 🌧️
⛅ Cumulus — puffy, cotton-like, below 2,000m — no rain but can → cumulonimbus (storms) ⛈️
🪶 Cirrus — wispy, HIGHEST (>6,000m), made of ICE CRYSTALS ❄️
🧊 Sleet = melted snowflakes REFREEZE into ice pellets falling through cold surface air
⚽ Hail = water droplets carried HIGH by thunderstorm updrafts, freeze, grow heavy, fall fast
🌀

Hurricane Science: The Coriolis Effect is why hurricanes in the Northern Hemisphere always spin counterclockwise, while typhoons in the Southern Hemisphere spin clockwise. Fun test: water going down a drain actually CAN spin differently depending on hemisphere — though in a bathtub the effect is tiny!
